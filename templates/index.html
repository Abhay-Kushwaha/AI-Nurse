Talking Baby

Typhoid fever, caused by Salmonella Typhi bacteria, presents with symptoms like a persistent high fever, fatigue, abdominal pain, and potentially a rash. Here's a more detailed look at the symptoms of typhoid fever: Early Symptoms (1-3 weeks after exposure): Fever: A gradual increase in fever, potentially reaching as high as 103-104°F (39-40°C). Fatigue and Weakness: Feeling generally unwell, with persistent tiredness and a lack of energy.
